3.5 ChatGPT Growth

Section 3: AI Sensations Reading time: 7 minutes By Thorium-AI Team

The phenomenon of ChatGPT's growth is not just a success story of a tech product. It is a case study of the unprecedented speed and scale of adoption of a new paradigm for human-machine interaction. The growth from 0 to 100 million active users in two months (November 2022 – January 2023) is a figure that forever changed the benchmark for launching any consumer technology in the future.

Timeline of Explosive Growth

  • November 30, 2022: Official launch of ChatGPT based on the GPT-3.5 model as a "free research preview."
  • December 4: 1 million users.
  • End of December: Over 10 million users. The product goes viral on Twitter, LinkedIn, and in academic and creative circles.

End of January 2023: 100 million monthly active users. For comparison: TikTok took 9 months post-launch, Instagram – 2.5 years, Netflix – 3.5 years, Spotify – 4.5 years to reach the same milestone.

2023-2024: Growth continues, though it slows, reaching ~180 million users by mid-2024. The key becomes not just quantity, but depth of penetration—ChatGPT becomes an everyday tool for hundreds of professions.

Anatomy of Virality: Why Was the Growth So Fast?

Zero Barrier to Entry and Immediate Utility.

No registration, installation, or training required. Simply visit the website and start a dialogue in natural language. This was radically different from the complex APIs or developer tools that existed before.

Instant Value: Within the first seconds, a user could get a coherent answer to a complex question, a plan, code, a poem, a business idea. This created a "wow" effect and a desire to share.

Self-Replicating Content (The Product as Its Own Marketing).

ChatGPT was perfectly suited for the social media era. People didn't just talk about the product—they published its outputs: conversations, poems, code, images (via DALL-E integration), philosophical reasoning.

Each such post was live, personalized advertising demonstrating concrete utility. This created a loop: post → new users → new posts.

Cross-Sector Application.

The product simultaneously appealed to dozens of audiences: students (help with essays), programmers (writing and debugging code), marketers and copywriters (text generation), businesspeople (analysis, plans), the curious (answers to any questions), creative individuals (ideas, scripts).

This meant viral spread occurred not through one channel, but through all professional and social graphs simultaneously.

Perfect Launch Timing (Product-Market-Timing Fit).

  • Technological Readiness: By 2022, the public was "primed" through the image of AI in media (from Siri to DeepMind) but had no direct access to anything this powerful.
  • Social Fatigue: Fatigue from algorithmic feeds, clickbait, and the complexity of finding information. ChatGPT offered an alternative—direct, contextual dialogue.
  • Economic Context: A wave of tech layoffs, general uncertainty. People were looking for tools to boost personal efficiency, and ChatGPT instantly positioned itself as such a "super-assistant."

Growth Stages and Perception Shift

The "Toy/Wonder" Phase (First weeks): Users tested the limits: asking for poems in Shakespeare's style, explaining quantum physics to a five-year-old, generating scripts. The main driver was curiosity and entertainment.

The "Tool" Phase (January – March 2023): Mass realization of practical utility. Students start using it for studies, developers for work. The first scandals arise (plagiarism, bans in schools and companies), which only fuel interest. The launch of the paid ChatGPT Plus tier at $20/month legitimizes the product as a professional service and creates the first steady revenue stream.

The "Platform/Ecosystem" Phase (From 2023):

  • API Launch: Allows any business to embed GPT's power into their products. Thousands of startups are built on this API.
  • Plugins and GPT Store Launch: Transforming ChatGPT from a monolithic chat into an extensible platform where specialized assistants can be created (e.g., for data analysis, travel planning, learning). This shifts growth from user count to the number of use cases and depth of integration into workflows.

Quantitative Growth Metrics: Beyond User Count

  • Website Traffic: At its peak (early 2023), chat.openai.com was among the top 30 most visited websites globally, surpassing Twitter, Netflix, and Instagram.
  • Revenue: Annualized run-rate revenue by the end of 2023 was estimated at $1.6+ billion, reaching this level less than a year after monetization. This is one of the fastest ascents to "unicorn" status ($1B+ ARR) in history.
  • Industry Impact: ChatGPT's growth directly triggered the "AI race," forcing Google, Meta, Microsoft, Amazon, and dozens of startups to urgently revise roadmaps and launch competing products (Bard, LLaMA, Copilot, etc.).

Factors Limiting and Altering Growth

Regulatory and Ethical Pressure: Bans in educational institutions, investigations into copyright infringement and data privacy violations. This forces OpenAI to introduce restrictions (e.g., on generating harmful content), which may slow viral growth but increases sustainability.

Competition: The emergence of free alternatives (Claude, Gemini, local models) creates an outflow of users sensitive to price or privacy.

Metric Shift: For OpenAI, what's more important now is not net new user growth, but retention of paying users, depth of API usage, and monetization of the corporate segment (ChatGPT Enterprise). Growth becomes qualitative, not quantitative.

Historical Significance

ChatGPT's growth is the point after which generative AI ceased to be a research domain and became a mass market. It demonstrated that there is a global, virtually unlimited demand for intelligence as a service. This was a crash test for infrastructure (servers crashed under load), social norms (what is honesty in education and creativity?), and business models.

This ascent proved that the key to mass adoption of a breakthrough technology is not only its power, but also unprecedented accessibility and interface simplicity. ChatGPT didn't just grow—it forever lowered the entry barrier into the AI era for a billion people, instantly making conversation with artificial intelligence as commonplace as a Google search.
